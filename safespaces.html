Physical Safe Space

Create a comforting environment that nurtures your well-being

  • Choose soft, warm lighting over harsh fluorescents
  • Add plants or natural elements for grounding
  • Keep comfort items within reach (blankets, pillows)
  • Minimize clutter for mental clarity
  • Use calming scents like lavender or vanilla
  • Display meaningful photos or artwork

Setting Boundaries

Protect your energy and peace of mind with healthy limits

  • Practice saying "no" without over-explaining
  • Set specific times for checking messages/emails
  • Create phone-free zones in your home
  • Limit time with energy-draining people
  • Communicate your needs clearly and kindly
  • Honor your own limits without guilt

Emotional Regulation

Tools to navigate and process your emotions safely

  • Practice deep breathing when overwhelmed
  • Name your emotions without judgment
  • Use journaling to process feelings
  • Try progressive muscle relaxation
  • Create a feelings vocabulary list
  • Practice self-compassion daily

Support Network

Build and maintain connections that uplift and support you

  • Identify trusted people you can talk to
  • Join supportive communities or groups
  • Practice vulnerability with safe people
  • Offer support to others when you can
  • Consider professional counseling support
  • Create regular check-ins with loved ones
