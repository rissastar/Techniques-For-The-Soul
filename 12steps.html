Sober Since:

12-Step Recovery Workbook
#SpeakLouderLoveHarder

Step 1: Honesty

We admitted we were powerless over our addiction - that our lives had become unmanageable.

This step is about facing the truth. It means recognizing that willpower alone cannot conquer addiction. Admitting powerlessness is not weakness—it's the beginning of healing.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What does powerlessness mean to you?
  • How has your life become unmanageable?
  • What fears come up when thinking about surrendering control?

Notes:

Step 2: Hope

Came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity.

This step is about believing that recovery is possible. Whether you believe in God, the universe, community, or inner strength, Step 2 invites you to open your heart to the possibility of healing.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What does “a Power greater than yourself” mean to you?
  • What has helped you feel hope in dark times?
  • How would your life look if you were restored to sanity?

Notes:

Step 3: Surrender

Made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him.

This step is not about blind faith. It’s about letting go of self-will and trusting a process bigger than us. It invites peace and humility.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What parts of your life are hardest to surrender?
  • What does trust look like for you?
  • How do you feel when you let go of control?

Notes:

Step 4: Courage

Made a searching and fearless moral inventory of ourselves.

This step asks us to look inward honestly. It's a deep dive into behaviors, resentments, fears, and harm done. It can be hard—but it’s powerful.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What patterns in your life cause pain?
  • What fears drive your actions?
  • What truths are you ready to face?

Notes:

Step 5: Integrity

Admitted to God, to ourselves, and to another human being the exact nature of our wrongs.

This step is about accountability and connection. Speaking your truth out loud is freeing. You are not alone.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What’s it like to speak your truth?
  • Who do you trust with your story?
  • What do you most need to admit?

Notes:

Step 6: Willingness

Were entirely ready to have God remove all these defects of character.

This step focuses on readiness for change—not perfection. It's a spiritual willingness to let go of old patterns, and open the door to transformation.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What behaviors are you willing to release?
  • What still gives you comfort, even if it harms you?
  • How ready are you to be free?

Notes:

Step 7: Humility

Humbly asked Him to remove our shortcomings.

This step is about letting go of pride and ego. It’s about opening yourself up to growth and change with a humble heart.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What does humility mean to you?
  • What gets in the way of asking for help?
  • How do you experience grace?

Notes:

Step 8: Forgiveness

Made a list of all persons we had harmed and became willing to make amends to them all.

This step is about recognizing the impact of our actions. It’s not about guilt—it’s about owning our part and being willing to heal relationships.

Reflection Prompts:

  • Who have you hurt through your addiction?
  • What would healing those relationships look like?
  • What does forgiveness (for others and yourself) mean?

Notes:

Step 9: Justice

Made direct amends to such people wherever possible, except when to do so would injure them or others.

Amends are more than apologies—they’re about taking responsibility and repairing trust. This step must be done with care and courage.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What amends are you ready to make?
  • How can you show you've changed?
  • How can you practice justice gently?

Notes:

Step 10: Perseverance

Continued to take personal inventory and when we were wrong promptly admitted it.

This step invites daily self-awareness. It’s a practice of checking in, correcting course, and growing in integrity over time.

Reflection Prompts:

  • How do you practice daily reflection?
  • What helps you admit when you're wrong?
  • How can you make self-inventory a habit?

Notes:

Step 11: Awareness

Sought through prayer and meditation to improve our conscious contact with God as we understood Him.

This step is about spiritual connection. It's a chance to deepen your inner life and stay grounded in peace, love, and purpose.

Reflection Prompts:

  • What does conscious contact mean to you?
  • How do you connect spiritually or mindfully?
  • What does your inner voice sound like?

Notes:

Step 12: Service

Having had a spiritual awakening as the result of these steps, we tried to carry this message to others and to practice these principles in all our affairs.

This step is about giving back and living the program. It’s about being of service, living your truth, and helping others on their journey.

Reflection Prompts:

  • How has your spirit changed during this process?
  • Who can you support in their healing?
  • What does recovery mean to you now?
