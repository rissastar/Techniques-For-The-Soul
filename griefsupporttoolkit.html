üè• When to Seek Emergency Help

Call 911 (US) or emergency services immediately if you or someone else:

  • Has thoughts of suicide or self-harm
  • Is experiencing severe depression or anxiety
  • Shows signs of psychosis or severe mental distress
  • Has substance abuse issues affecting safety
  • Is unable to care for themselves or others

    üåä Understanding Grief

    Grief is not a linear process. These stages are common experiences, but everyone grieves differently. You may experience them in any order, skip some, or revisit them multiple times.

    1. Denial

    Shock and disbelief help us cope with overwhelming loss. This is your mind's way of protecting you from the full impact of the loss.

    2. Anger

    Anger at the situation, others, or even your loved one is normal. It's often easier to feel angry than to feel the pain underneath.

    3. Bargaining

    Trying to make deals or negotiate (with God, universe, etc.) to undo the loss. "What if" and "if only" thoughts are common.

    4. Depression

    Deep sadness and despair as the reality of loss sets in. This is a natural response to significant loss, not a sign of weakness.

    5. Acceptance

    Not "being okay" with the loss, but accepting the reality of it. Finding ways to move forward while honoring your loved one's memory.

    üéØ Coping Strategies by Stage

    During Denial:

    • Be patient with yourself
    • Seek support from others
    • Allow yourself to feel numb
    • Don't rush the process

    During Anger:

    • Exercise or physical activity
    • Journaling or creative expression
    • Talk to trusted friends or counselors
    • Practice deep breathing

    During Bargaining:

    • Focus on what you can control
    • Practice mindfulness
    • Consider therapy or counseling
    • Connect with support groups

    During Depression:

    • Maintain basic self-care
    • Seek professional help if needed
    • Stay connected with supportive people
    • Be gentle with yourself

    During Acceptance:

    • Create meaningful rituals or memorials
    • Find ways to honor your loved one
    • Consider helping others in similar situations
    • Focus on rebuilding and moving forward
