Understanding Grief

Grief is a deeply personal and natural response to loss. It affects us emotionally, physically, and mentally. Everyone experiences grief differently, and there is no right or wrong way to grieve.

Learn about the stages of grief

The five common stages of grief are:

  • Denial: Difficulty accepting the reality of loss.
  • Anger: Feeling frustrated or helpless.
  • Bargaining: Trying to negotiate or make deals to reverse loss.
  • Depression: Deep sadness and reflection.
  • Acceptance: Finding peace and moving forward.

These stages are not linear and can overlap or come and go.

Coping Strategies

When grief feels overwhelming, these gentle practices may help:

  • Practice deep breathing or mindfulness meditation.
  • Allow yourself to cry or express feelings freely.
  • Reach out to trusted friends or family members.
  • Set small daily goals for self-care.
  • Take gentle walks or spend time in nature.

Self-Care Reminders

  • Be gentle and patient with yourself.
  • Rest when your body and mind need it.
  • Eat nourishing food, even if in small amounts.
  • Find moments of quiet and peace each day.
